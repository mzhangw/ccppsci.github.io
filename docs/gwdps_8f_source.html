23!> \section gen_gwdps GFS Orographic GWD Scheme General Algorithm
24!! -# Calculate subgrid mountain blocking
25!! -# Calculate orographic wave drag
26!!
27!! The NWP model gravity wave drag (GWD) scheme in the GFS has two
28!! main components: how the surface stress is computed, and then how
29!! that stress is distributed over a vertical column where it may
30!! interact with the models momentum. Each of these depends on the
31!! large scale environmental atmospheric state and assumptions about
32!! the sub-grid scale processes. In Alpert GWD (1987) based on linear,
33!! two-dimensional non-rotating, stably stratified flow over a mountain ridge,
34!! sub-grid scale gravity wave motions are assumed which propagate away
35!! from the mountain. Described in Alpert (1987), the flux measured over
36!! a "low level" vertically averaged layer, in the atmosphere defines a base
37!! level flux. "Low level" was taken to be the first 1/3 of the troposphere
38!! in the 1987 implementation. This choice was meant to encompass a thick
39!! low layer for vertical averages of the environmental (large scale) flow
40!! quantities. The vertical momentum flux or gravity wave stress in a
41!! grid box due to a single mountain is given as in Pierrehumbert(1986)
42!! \cite pierrehumbert_1986 :
43!! \f[
44!! \tau = \frac {\rho \: U^{3}\: G(F_{r})} {\Delta X \; N }
45!! \f]
46!! where \f$ \Delta X \f$ is a grid increment, N is the Brunt Viasala frequency
47!! \f[
48!! N(\sigma) = \frac{-g \: \sigma \:
49!! \frac{\partial\Theta}{\partial\sigma}}{\Theta \:R \:T}
50!! \f]
51!! The environmental variables are calculated from a mass weighted vertical
52!! average over a base layer. \f$G(F_{r})\f$ is a monotonically increasing
53!! function of Froude number :
54!! \f[
55!! F_{r} = \frac{N h^{'}}{U}
56!! \f]
57!! where \f$U\f$ is the wind speed calculated as a mass weighted vertical average in
58!! the base layer, and \f$h^{'}\f$, is the vertical displacement caused by the orography
59!! variance. An effective mountain length for the gravity wave processes:
60!! \f[
61!! l^{*} = \frac{\Delta X}{m}
62!! \f]
63!! where \f$m\f$ is the number of mountains in a grid box, can then
64!! be defined to obtain the form of the base level stress
65!! \f[
66!! \tau = \frac {\rho \: U^{3} \: G(F_{r})} {N \;l^{*}}
67!! \f]
68!! giving the stress induced from the surface in a model grid box.
69!! Pierrehumbert(1986) \cite pierrehumbert_1986 gives the form
70!! for the function \f$G(F_{r})\f$ as
71!! \f[
72!! G(F_{r}) = \bar{G}\frac{F^{2}_{r}}{F^{2}_{r}\: + \:a^{2}}
73!! \f]
74!! Where \f$ \bar{G} \f$ is an order unity non-dimensional saturation
75!! flux set to 1 and \f$a\f$ is a function of the mountain aspect ratio also
76!!set to 1 in the 1987 implementation of the GFS GWD. Typical values of
77!! U=10m/s, N=0.01 1/s, l*=100km, and a=1, gives a flux of 1 Pascal and
78!! if this flux is made to go to zero linearly with height then the
79!! decelerations would be about 10/m/s/day which is consistent with
80!! observations in PH.
81!!
82!! In Kim, Moorthi, Alpert's (1998, 2001) GWD currently in GFS operations,
83!! the GWD scheme has the same physical basis as in Alpert (1987) with the addition
84!! of enhancement factors for the amplitude, G, and mountain shape details
85!! in G(Fr) to account for effects from the mountain blocking. A factor,
86!! E m', is an enhancement factor on the stress in the Alpert '87 scheme.
87!! The E ranges from no enhancement to an upper limit of 3, E=E(OA)[1-3],
88!! and is a function of OA, the Orographic Asymmetry defined in Kim and Arakawa (1995)
89!! \cite kim_and_arakawa_1995 as
90!! Orographic Asymmetry (OA):
91!! \f[
92!! OA=\frac{ \bar{x} \; - \;
93!! \sum\limits_{j=1}^{N_{b}} x_{j} \; n_{j} }{\sigma_{x}}
94!! \f]
95!! where \f$N_{b}\f$ is the total number of bottom blocks in the mountain barrier,
96!! \f$ \sigma_{x} \f$ is the standard deviation of the horizontal distance defined by
97!!\f[
98!! \sigma_{x} = \sqrt{ \frac{\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N_{b}}
99!! \; (x_{j} \; - \; \bar{x} )^2}{N_{x}} }
100!!\f]
101!! where \f$N_{x}\f$ is the number of grid intervals for the large scale domain being
102!! considered. So the term, E(OA)m'/ \f$ \Delta X \f$ in Kim's scheme represents
103!! a multiplier on G shown in Alpert's eq (1), where m' is the number of mountains
104!! in a sub-grid scale box. Kim increased the complexity of m' making it a
105!! function of the fractional area of the sub-grid mountain and the asymmetry
106!! and convexity statistics which are found from running a gravity wave
107!! model for a large number of cases:
108!! \f[
109!! m^{'} = C_{m} \Delta X \left[ \frac{1 \; + \;
110!! \sum\limits_{x} L_{h} }{\Delta X} \right]^{OA+1}
111!! \f]
112!! Where, according to Kim, \f$ \sum \frac{L_{h}}{\Delta X} \f$ is
113!! the fractional area covered by the subgrid-scale orography higher than
114!! a critical height \f$ h_{c} = Fr_{c} U_{0}/N_{0} \f$ , over the
115!! "low level" vertically averaged layer, for a grid box with the interval
116!! \f$ \Delta X \f$. Each \f$ L_{n}\f$ is the width of a segment of
117!! orography intersection at the critical height:
118!! \f[
119!! Fr_{0} = \frac{N_{0} \; h^{'}}{U_{0}}
120!! \f]
121!! \f[
122!! G^{'}(OC,Fr_{0}) = \frac{Fr_{0}^{2}}{Fr_{0}^{2} \; + \; a^{2}}
123!! \f]
124!! \f[
125!! a^{2} = \frac{C_{G}}{OC}
126!! \f]
127!! \f[
128!! E(OA, Fr_{0}) = (OA \; + \; 2)^{\delta}
129!! \f]
130!! and
131!! \f[
132!! \delta \; = \; \frac{C_{E} \; Fr_{0}}{Fr_{c}}
133!! \f]
134!! where \f$ Fr_{c} \f$ is as in Alpert.
135!!
136!! This represents a closed scheme, somewhat empirical adjustments
137!! to the original scheme to calculate the surface stress.
138!!
139!! Momentum is deposited by the sub-grid scale gravity waves break due
140!! to the presence of convective mixing assumed to occur when the
141!! minimum Richardson number:
142!!
143!! Orographic Convexity (OC) =
144!! \f[
145!! OC=\frac{ \sum\limits_{j=1}^{N_{x}}
146!! \; (h_{j} \; - \; \bar{h})^4 }{N_{x} \;\sigma_{h}^4}
147!! \f]
148!! and where
149!! \f[
150!! \sigma_{h} = \sqrt{ \frac{\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N_{x}}
151!! \; (h_{j} \; - \; \bar{h} )^2}{N_{x}} }
152!! \f]
153!! This represents a closed scheme, somewhat empirical adjustments
154!! to the original scheme to calculate the surface stress.
155!!
156!! Momentum is deposited by the sub-grid scale gravity waves break due
157!! to the presence of convective mixing assumed to occur when
158!! the minimum Richardson number:
159!! \f[
160!! Ri_{m} = \frac{Ri(1 \; - \; Fr)}{(1 \; + \; \sqrt{Ri}Fr)^2}
161!! \f]
162!! Is less than 1/4 Or if critical layers are encountered in a layer
163!! the the momentum flux will vanish. The critical layer is defined
164!! when the base layer wind becomes perpendicular to the environmental
165!! wind. Otherwise, wave breaking occurs at a level where the amplification
166!! of the wave causes the local Froude number or similarly a truncated
167!! (first term of the) Scorer parameter, to be reduced below a critical
168!! value by the saturation hypothesis (Lindzen). This is done through
169!! eq 1 which can be written as
170!! \f[
171!! \tau = \rho U N k h^{'2}
172!! \f]
173!! For small Froude number this is discretized in the vertical so at each
174!! level the stress is reduced by ratio of the Froude or truncated Scorer
175!! parameter, \f$ \frac{U^{2}}{N^{2}} = \frac{N \tau_{l-1}}{\rho U^{3} k} \f$ ,
176!! where the stress is from the layer below beginning with that found near
177!! the surface. The respective change in momentum is applied in
178!! that layer building up from below.
179!!
180!! An amplitude factor is part of the calibration of this scheme which is
181!! a function of the model resolution and the vertical diffusion. This
182!! is because the vertical diffusion and the GWD account encompass
183!! similar physical processes. Thus, one needs to run the model over
184!! and over for various amplitude factors for GWD and vertical diffusion.
185!!
186!! In addition, there is also mountain blocking from lift and frictional
187!! forces. Improved integration between how the GWD is calculated and
188!! the mountain blocking of wind flow around sub-grid scale orography
189!! is underway at NCEP. The GFS already has convectively forced GWD
190!! an independent process.
191!!
